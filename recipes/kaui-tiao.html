Fried Rice

Ingredients(Broth)

  • Pork neck bones
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Star anise
  • Coriander seeds
  • Galangal
  • Cilantro roots or cilantro stems
  • Daikon
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • White pepper
  • Pandan leaf
  • Soy sauce
  • Golden Mountain Sauce
  • Taojiew
  • Thai black soy sauce or dark soy sauce
  • Fish sauce
  • White vinegar
  • Rock sugar or granulated sugar
  • Liquid beef or pork blood

Ingredients (Bowl)

  • Pork shoulder,
  • Dry rice noodles
  • Asian style pork or beef meatballs
  • Water spinach or spinach
  • Bean sprouts
  • Cilantro and/or green onions
  • Thai basil or holy basil (optional)
  • Small dry rice noodles (sen lek)

Step by step

  1. Simmer the pork bones in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, toast the dry spices until slightly charred.
  3. Put the spices in a soup infusion bag of cheesecloth, along with the galangal and cilantro roots/stems.
  4. After 30 mins, skim the scum from the broth.
  5. Add the spice bag, daikon, onion, garlic, pepper, pandan leaf (tie it into a knot) and all of the seasonings except salt. Simmer gently for 1 hr 15 mins, topping it up with water as needed to keep the bones completely submerged.
  6. Meanwhile combine pork shoulder slices with soy sauce and sugar and set aside.
  7. When the broth is done, remove the vegetables (can be eaten), spice bag (discard), and pork bones. Pick off any meat from the bones and reserve. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt as needed.
  8. Bring the broth to a simmer and place the pork shoulder in a sieve or wire skimmer and dunk it into the broth, stirring it around until cooked, this should take less than 1 minute. Lift it up from the broth and set aside.
  9. Bring the broth back to a boil then gradually add the blood WHILE STIRRING and watch the broth thicken! Add the meatballs and keep the broth hot on low heat and covered, until ready to serve.
  10. Bring a LARGE pot of water to a FULL boil, place 1 portion of the soaked rice noodles, bean sprouts and water spinach in a noodle strainer or sieve, and blanch for 5 seconds (shake it around in the water) then place into serving bowl. (If using other kinds of noodles, see noodle options section for instructions.)
  11. Ladle the broth along with a few meatballs over the noodles.
  12. Top with marinated pork, meat from bones, and all the toppings and condiments as desired. Enjoy!
