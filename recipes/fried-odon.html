Nestled in the heart of Tokyo's bustling streets, "Sakura Soba" was renowned not for its soba but for Chef Hiroto's exquisite Sakura Udon. Made with handcrafted udon noodles bathed in a broth simmered to perfection with bonito flakes and kombu, each bowl was a testament to Hiroto's culinary finesse. The dish, adorned with fresh vegetables and tender pork slices, captivated all who tasted it with its harmonious blend of flavors.

When a curious traveler stumbled upon Sakura Soba and tasted Hiroto's udon, they were immediately entranced. The delicate texture of the noodles, the depth of the broth, and the freshness of the ingredients spoke volumes of Hiroto's dedication to his craft. Soon, word of Sakura Udon spread across Tokyo like wildfire, drawing crowds eager to experience the sublime simplicity and profound flavors of Hiroto's signature dish, making Sakura Soba a beloved culinary destination in the heart of the city.

Ingredients

  • Udon noodle
  • Carrots
  • Spring onion
  • Dark soy sauce
  • Light soy sauce
  • Sesame oil
  • Sugar
  • chicken or other protein of choice

Step by step

  1. Boil some water in a large saucepan. Add 250ml cold water and the udon noodles. (As they are so thick, adding cold water helps them to cook a little bit slower so the middle cooks through). If using frozen or fresh noodles, cook for 2 mins or until al dente; dried will take longer, about 5-6 mins. Drain and leave in the colander.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp of the oil, add the onion and cabbage and sauté for 5 mins until softened. Add the mushrooms and some spring onions, and sauté for 1 more min. Pour in the remaining sesame oil and the noodles. If using cold noodles, let them heat through before adding the ingredients for the sauce – otherwise tip in straight away and keep stir-frying until sticky and piping hot. Sprinkle with the remaining spring onions.
